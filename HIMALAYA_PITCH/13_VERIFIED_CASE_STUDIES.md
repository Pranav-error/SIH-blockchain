## 🥬 CASE STUDY 1: WALMART - Leafy Greens Traceability

### Background
In 2018, a deadly E. coli outbreak linked to romaine lettuce killed 5 people and hospitalized 200+ in the US. Walmart couldn't trace the source for **weeks**.

### Implementation
- **Technology:** IBM Food Trust (Hyperledger Fabric) - Same technology as HerBlock
- **Scope:** All leafy green suppliers (100+ suppliers)
- **Timeline:** Mandatory by September 2019

### Results (Verified)

| Metric | Before Blockchain | After Blockchain | Source |
|--------|------------------|------------------|--------|
| **Trace Time** | 7 days | **2.2 seconds** | IBM Press Release, 2018 |
| **Data Points** | Manual entry | Real-time automated | Walmart Investor Report |
| **Recall Precision** | Entire category | **Specific farm/batch** | FDA Report 2020 |

### Key Quote
> "What used to take us 7 days to trace, now takes 2.2 seconds. This is a game-changer for food safety."
> — **Frank Yiannas**, VP Food Safety, Walmart (now FDA Deputy Commissioner)

### Source Links
- IBM News: https://newsroom.ibm.com/2018-09-24-Walmart-Inc-Frank-Yiannas
- Harvard Business School Case Study: "Walmart's Food Safety Solution"
